Javier Hernández Balcázar

Religion

Hernández is a devout Catholic.

Political Views

Hernández is largely non-political except for his work with Mexican children via the United Nations.

Javier Hernández Balcázar was born and raised in Guadalajara, Jalisco, Mexico.

Hernández is a devout Catholic.1 He is even known for his ritual pre-game prayers, in which, in front of a crowd of thousands, Hernández will kneel down on the field, hold his hands at chest level, and chant a prayer.2

This usually isn’t a problem. But when Hernández’ team plays the Glasgow, Scotland Rangers–a team known for its anti-Catholic sentiment and roots in the Anglican tradition–Hernández is asked to avoid his prayers for the sake of peace.3

But Hernández isn’t fazed, and is known to be a grounded, spiritual and dedicated person and player who doesn’t participate in his teammates’ late night parties and has been with the same girlfriend for years. His grandfather said of Hernández:

He has his diet — he doesn’t eat just anything. He never drinks even a drop of alcohol. He doesn’t stay up late for any reason.4

Sounds like he’s a bit of a goody-two-shoes.

International football politics

Footballers are international entities, bounced around the world from football club to football club, country to country, city to city. And clearly, Hernández holds a special place in his heart for his home country of Mexico. As an international figure, his quasi-political activities have been for the benefit of Mexico through an international organization–the United Nations.

Hernández is a UNICEF ambassador in Mexico, charged with promoting, encouraging and helping to bring a better life to Mexican children through education, nutrition, and protection from diseases and social problems.5 This could easily be viewed as charitable/philanthropic activities rather then political activities.

I can’t find anything about direct political statements or involvement from Hernández. Maybe if I knew Spanish, I could. Any Hernández fans out there that know anything, let us know in the comments.
