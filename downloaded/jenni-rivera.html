Jenni Rivera

Religion

Rivera was a Catholic, although she said she would have liked to be a better Christian.

Political Views

She was a vocal advocate for immigrants, women, and LGBT youth.

Dolores Janney Rivera, known as Jenni Rivera, was born and raised in Long Beach, California. She died in a plane crash in 2012.

As the child of Mexican immigrants to the U.S., it’s not surprising that Rivera was Catholic. And her family is very devout. After her death, the singer’s brother said,

Life is like that. We live and we die. We may be sad, but when God has the last word for all of us in our last days, it’s time to go. And this was the way Jenni had to go.1

Rivera herself said she wasn’t quite as dedicated to her religion as she would like to be,2 but she still obviously had a strong faith in God. She said she was reading a book by Christian evangelist Billy Graham,3 and she had this to say about playing gigs in Mexican bordertowns racked with violence from drug cartels:

A lot of my friends, colleagues in the business, have been kidnapped or murdered or whatever. . . . The best thing that I can do is pray and ask God to give me grace while I’m onstage and get me safely back home.4

That certainly takes some faith.

There’s no doubt we would have heard more from this woman if her life had not been cut short, but her impact on Mexican-American culture will no doubt last much longer.
