Charice

Religion

Charice is a recent Catholic convert.

Political Views

Charice is generally non-political unless you count how proud her country is of her.

Charice, whose full name is Charmaine Clarice Relucio Pempengco, was born and raised in Laguna, Philippines.

Charice is a Catholic, but this is a rather recent development–officially at least. With an excited Catholic na ako! (Filipino for “I am Catholic!”) Charice was baptized and confirmed into the Catholic Church on her 18th birthday.1 It was no ordinary baptism either. Attending were dozens of the biggest Filipino stars as well as a camera crew and representatives from Oprah Winfrey and representatives of record producer David Foster.2 Watch the video here.

But why wasn’t Charice baptized and confirmed at a much younger age–as is customary in the Catholic Church? According to her mother, they didn’t have the money (which seems strange as I believe it’s a free service) and that they didn’t have a complete family.3 Charice recounts the story of her estranged father:

…my father came home and started screaming at my mother. And then he held a gun to her head. Our neighbors broke in and helped us get out… That was the last I ever saw of him.4

Perhaps Charice’s newly-found cadre of celebrities now makes up for her late father.

A, shall we say, cultural attach√©

Charice isn’t too political, but she’s had her moments.

For the most part, she is considered, by Filipinos, a symbol of pride on the world’s stage. She is listed among Filipinos like Manny Pacquiaos and Jessica Sanchezes as a Filipino who’s making the country look good to Americans,5 is mentioned during political debates in her home country (for presumably the same reason), and is used as an excuse to ask the Filipino government for more money for the arts.6

But she seems fairly unconcerned with the true political workings of her home country–or of the rest of the world. Well, unless you count her singing answers to the U.S. citizenship test to a frustrated, would-be American in the film Here Comes the Boom. Check out the clip here.
