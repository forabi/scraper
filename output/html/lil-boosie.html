Lil Boosie

Religion

Lil Boosie's strong Christian belief comes through rather darkly in his lyrics.

Political Views

He's non-political mostly through his distrust of government.

Lil Boosie, whose real name is Torrence Hatch, was born and raised in Baton Rouge, Louisiana.

God and Jesus and Christianity are all over Boosie’s lyrics. But it’s not so much about thanking God for blessings and praying for fellow man, like you might see in pop music. His raps are dark, and the religious stuff is just as dark as the rest. Take his song “Betrayed” for example. Here’s the hook:

Father God I’ve been betrayed/ Feel like they beat me like a slave/ But I learn from my mistakes/ We living in the last days/ Pray for me and make me cold hearted1

Pretty heavy. Boosie is currently, as of the writing of this article, in a federal prison, so there are fewer interviews from him than there might be otherwise. But an interview with his mother revealed he has some religious roots–his maternal grandfather was a minister,2 although she didn’t state a denomination. (Baptist would be a good bet, though.34 )

While in prison for drug charges, Boosie was also charged with first degree murder, of which he was later found not guilty.5 Before the trial, his mother made clear she believed her son was not a criminal. She said,

When it’s time for God to come back, he’s going to judge us, not for our color, not for our character, it’s our heart.6

We don’t need you on our streets

Boosie’s ability to vote is going to be on hold for a few years while he deals with his whole felony thing. But he doesn’t strike me as one who gets really excited about engaging in the democratic process. Boosie has more distrust for government than anything else–especially when it comes to the executive authority of police officers and prosecutors.

First there’s his song “Fuck ‘Em All” where he says, “Fuck the D.A. and the prosecutors, they all devils,” and then states his hope that they get it “in the ass with ten dicks.”7

And then there’s his song, “Fuck the Police” where the hook is:

We don’t need you bitches on our street say with me/ Fuck the police! Fuck the police!/ Without that badge you a bitch and a half nigga8

Not only were lyrics like those suspected as a reason Boosie’s drug sentence was so stiff, but his lyrics were also used against him in his murder trial. But apparently a jury didn’t think that was evidence enough.9

Maybe Boosie’s more of an anarchist than anything else, but probably he’s just non-political. If he starts speaking up about prison reform, drug legalization, or gun control when he gets out of jail, keep us updated in the comments.
