Sara Jay

Religion

Sara Jay is agnostic and non-religious.

Political Views

She's liberal, and is especially focused on combating internet piracy (a.k.a, free porn).

Sara Jay was born and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio.

Jay is agnostic1 and entirely non-religious. Her non-belief seems to manifest itself around Christmas-time, a holiday she really really dislikes.

[Christmas] is about Jesus and his birthday. I have no reason to celebrate that because I simply am not part of that. I don’t celebrate Haunakka or Kwanza, or any other holiday that does not relate directly to me.2

She continues by railing on the commercialization of the holiday and says she refuses to take part in it. So maybe she just uses the religion thing as an excuse to ditch the stress of the holidays–but either way, she seems to take the non-religious thing seriously.

Obama, Guns, and Internet Piracy

Jay has a lot of opinions, it’s just a matter of finding an interviewer who cares enough to ask her. (Interviews of porn stars aren’t always super intellectual affairs.) From what I found, Jay leans to the left.

First of all, she and Obama seem to be on good terms. For a time, Obama was allegedly following Sara Jay on Twitter, although I can’t imagine his advisers let that last for very long. Whether or not she voted for him, she was pretty stoked: she said she almost choked on her cereal when she found out.3

Jay supports repealing Arizona’s immigration law, SB1070, and she thinks there should be tighter regulations on guns. She supports the right to bear arms, but expressed concern about size of gun she was able to purchase.

I kind of feel like I shouldn’t have been able to buy that. Like, what do you need a shotgun like that for. . . . I’m not a big fan of guns at all.4

The biggest issue for her, though, is internet piracy. Understandably so. When people download illegal free porn, she doesn’t get paid. She’s even looking into the issue with her lawyers to see what can be done. But she’s not counting on the government to help her out. On why the music industry gets governmental support with the issue, and the porn industry doesn’t, she says,

It’s a big conservative thing. People don’t find as much value in porn as they do in music. And with the conservative background of our government’s history, they’re not going to put their limbs out for us.5

Yeah, I can’t imagine any politician touching that with a ten-foot pole. I guess Sara Jay will have to work it out on her own for now.
